# Design research: Hire Assistant Near Me

## Search brief

**Buyer-intent query:** `hire virtual assistant services near me`

The query mixes local intent with a service that can be delivered remotely. The homepage should answer that tension immediately: explain what truly needs a nearby assistant, what can be handled remotely, and offer a managed route for owners who do not want to screen alone.

## Five direct organic competitors inspected

### 1. BELAY

URL: https://belaysolutions.com/services/assistant/virtual

BELAY uses a navy and mint editorial system, a right-aligned human hero image, a prominent intake form, and extensive reassurance around U.S.-based talent and matching. It feels established, but the hero is copy-heavy and the page repeats too many conversion paths.

Useful pattern: lead with relief, pair a real person with the offer, and explain the managed match.

### 2. Time etc

URL: https://www.timeetc.com/

Time etc uses a blue split hero, green CTA, workplace photography, longevity metrics, review badges, and concrete task categories. The message is friendly and easy to grasp. Some sections are oversized or appear empty, and the main CTA sounds more like a content quiz than a hiring step.

Useful pattern: keep one low-friction route for visitors who are still deciding what to delegate.

### 3. Wing Assistant

URL: https://wingassistant.com/

Wing has the densest proof stack: ratings, customer logos, direct pricing access, fit/not-fit guidance, compliance, role cards, and case studies. The blue-purple gradient system feels modern, though the hero is visually empty on the right and the very long page repeats the same ownership message.

Useful pattern: qualify the buyer honestly and make managed support distinct from a freelance marketplace.

### 4. Prialto

URL: https://www.prialto.com/

Prialto uses a charcoal hero, teal and orange accents, a collage of real assistant portraits, and customer logos directly below the fold. Its people/process/technology story is credible for enterprise buyers. The strongest differentiator, cross-trained backup support, arrives too late.

Useful pattern: show the service as a team and system rather than one isolated contractor.

### 5. Boldly

URL: https://boldly.com/

Boldly has the strongest visual direction. The editorial serif type, warm off-white canvas, coral CTAs, cutout portraits, and named relationship framing make the service feel premium and human. Its copy has personality without looking like standard staffing-site copy. The main weaknesses are huge empty sections and a single high-intent CTA for every visitor.

Useful pattern: editorial confidence, real-person imagery, restrained color, and fit-first messaging.

## Selected direction

**Winner: Boldly, adapted into a local assistant finder.**

The new design combines Boldly's premium editorial tone with the browsable clarity of a local marketplace. It uses a warm cream canvas, ink typography, persimmon accents, a location-led hero, assistant-role cards, local-market examples, and a plain local-versus-remote decision section.

The homepage should feel like a helpful local staffing desk, not an offshore template and not a directory pretending to list specific workers. The image cards describe support categories rather than invented people. Planning ranges are labeled as guidance, not measured outcomes.

## Originality notes

The implementation does not copy competitor copy, logos, illustrations, layouts, or brand assets. The location rail, map-pin logo, `nearby / remote / managed` decision framework, task brief, city examples, and card composition were created for HireAssistantNearMe.com.

## Copy direction

- Use "near me" in the H1 naturally, then explain that proximity can mean shared hours and reliable handoffs, not only the same ZIP code.
- Avoid claims about ratings, customers, placement speed, screening volume, or savings unless the site has evidence.
- Do not publish prices.
- Do not invent candidate names, testimonials, employer logos, or certifications.
- Keep the voice direct: "Keep the office work local. Move the laptop work off your plate."
- Use simple sentences and varied rhythm. Avoid generic staffing phrases such as "unlock your potential," "seamless support," and "transform your business."

## Desktop QA target

At 1440px, the first screen should show the full navigation, H1, both CTA choices, the location brief, a strong office/team photo, and the opening of the role strip. No image may collide with copy, no navigation labels may run together, and the hero must remain the first meaningful visual.
